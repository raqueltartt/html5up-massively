ABOUT ME

Raquel Tartt is an honors psychology student at Howard University dedicated to understanding people, systems, and the forces that shape both. Her research interests and professional experience sits at the intersection of mental health, education policy, and prison reform, where she explore how inequities in these areas impact marginalized communities. Through research in paralanguage and hostile attribution bias, prison mentorship through Howard University Youth Justice Advocates (YJA) and the Petey Green Program, peer counseling through the Howard University Peer Counseling Program (HUPCP), and leadership within the Howard Chapter of the NAACP and the College of Arts and Sciences Honors Association (CASHA), She strives to create spaces that prioritize healing, growth, and equitable opportunity. She is is passionate about turning insight into action—whether that means supporting justice-involved youth, advancing conversations around mental health, or advocating for more equitable education systems. This space reflects my commitment to meaningful change, intentional community building, and the belief that better systems begin with deeper understanding .
